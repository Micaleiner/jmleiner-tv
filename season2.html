The Premise

Abigail Fisher — now operating under her chosen surname, relocated to Forest Hills, New York — takes a senior finance role at Cinevision AdTech and steps onto a board already in motion. What looks like a variance becomes a pattern. What looks like a correction becomes evidence. The more she understands, the more her own name binds her to the system she is trying to expose.

The Crime

Sebastian Carter has built a financial architecture designed to look like control. Across nine years, minor adjustments, subsidiary accounts, and acquisition timing conceal a system no one is meant to see. Abigail does not uncover a theft. She uncovers a machine.

The Chess Architecture

Each character occupies a chess piece. Each piece dictates how they move. Across nine episodes, the same ritual repeats: office, glass, pour, watch, hand at the keyboard. Nine years of pressure. One board no one fully sees.

The Queen

Abigail Fisher

Most powerful, most mobile, most at risk. Abigail can move through the system faster than anyone else — but every discovery makes her more useful to a game she does not yet know she is playing.

The Bishop

Lukas Moreau

Never moves far. Never exposed. Lukas reads the board before anyone else admits there is one. He lets other people open the files, ask the questions, and clear the square he wants.

The King

Sebastian Carter

Never moves straight. Built the board before anyone else understood the game. Nine years of ritual. One position with no clean move left.

The Rook

Blake Keaton

Powerful in straight lines. Solid, defensive, vulnerable because he still believes decency can survive inside a building designed to punish it.

The Knight

Liam Carrington

L-shaped, unpredictable, able to jump every boundary because he never understands the danger he carries. Liam is not strategy. He is disruption.

  

The Pawns

Vanessa · Mason · Lena

Expended. Moved by others. Lena documented the system, knew she was not coming back, left a ghost archive — IC_recon_legacy_v1.wk1 — and planted a backdoor. Abigail's regulatory submission is named simply: LENA.
