Five stand-alone psychological suspense stories about deception, power, survival, and one woman's refusal to stop reading what others hope will remain hidden.

The Authority Behind the Work

Johanna Marica Leiner brings twenty years of professional experience inside the systems her work examines. She has worked in corporate finance, technology and advertising, creative industries, and hospitality management—across multiple continents and institutional contexts. This is not observation from the outside. It is testimony from someone who has operated inside these systems, understood their architecture, and watched how they conceal harm through procedure, policy, and the careful deployment of charm.

That accumulated knowledge informs every institutional detail in The Deception Anthology: the way meetings are run, the way information is controlled, the way ambition distorts loyalty, the way systems protect themselves. The characters in this anthology behave like real people inside real institutions because they are built on patterns Leiner has recognized across decades of professional life.

Its worlds are fictional. Its understanding of how systems conceal harm is not.

Season One

Savile & Sage:
Fashion as Institution

The boutique at the heart of Season One is built from lived knowledge of design, craft, and the people who commission beauty. Leiner's background in bridal and fashion design informs every detail: the boutique's reputation, its clientele, the way aesthetic excellence becomes a structure for belonging—and a hiding place for something darker.

When Abi builds the boutique from nothing, she is not inventing the world—she is writing from memory of what it means to make something beautiful, to have people trust you with their most significant moments, and to discover that beauty can be weaponized.

Seasons Two Through Five

Coolum:
The Geography of Safety

Coolum Beach, Sunshine Coast, appears as the emotional anchor across the entire anthology. From Season Two onward, it is where Manon Davis lives, where Henry and Grace Fisher settle, where the family gathers, where Olivia marries on the beach at sunset.

This geography is built from intimate knowledge of running a guesthouse, managing events, orchestrating the details that make a space feel like home. The Coolum holiday house—where Mia and Damien will later retreat after his world collapses—is not invented. It is drawn from understanding what hospitality actually requires: attention to detail, emotional labor, the creation of safety for people you may never fully know.

Craft & Precision

Events & the
Architecture of Moments

Event management requires what the anthology requires: understanding how systems work, how people behave under pressure, how the smallest detail can unravel everything, and how to orchestrate complexity so it appears effortless.

The closing image of Season Two—Olivia's wedding at Coolum, the family gathered in golden light, every element aligned—is written from knowledge of what it takes to create that moment, to hold it together, and to understand that beneath the beauty, the precision, the orchestration, there is always the awareness that systems can fail.
