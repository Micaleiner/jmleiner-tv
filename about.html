Five stand-alone psychological suspense stories about deception, power, survival, and one woman’s refusal to stop reading what others hope will remain hidden.

Created and written by Johanna Marica Leiner, The Deception Anthology is shaped by lived knowledge of corporate, creative, and institutional systems.

The work is fiction. Its characters and institutions are invented. But its understanding of how systems conceal harm — through procedure, charm, status, silence, and control — comes from experience.

Its worlds are fictional. Its understanding of how systems conceal harm is not.

Across five stand-alone psychological suspense stories, that knowledge becomes drama: deception, power, survival, and one woman’s refusal to stop reading what others hope will remain hidden.

The anthology is not a documentary. It is a psychological suspense drama, comparable in tone and audience territory to Succession, Broadchurch, The Undoing, Anatomy of a Scandal, and Sharp Objects.
